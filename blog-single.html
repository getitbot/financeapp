5 Myths About Credit Scores Debunked

Parth Pande Jan 18, 2026 â€¢ 5 min read

Your credit score is your financial report card. Yet, misinformation about how it works is rampant. Let's clear the air and look at the facts.

Myth 1: Checking your own score lowers it.

This is the most common myth. Checking your own score is known as a "Soft Inquiry". It does not affect your score at all. You can check it daily if you want.

A "Hard Inquiry" only happens when a lender checks your score because you applied for a loan.

Myth 2: You need to carry a balance to build credit.

False. You do not need to pay interest to build credit. The best way to build credit is to pay off your balance in full every single month. This shows you are responsible without costing you a rupee.

Myth 3: Closing old cards improves your score.

Actually, it often hurts your score. Your credit history length makes up 15% of your score. Closing an old card shortens your average history and reduces your total available credit limit.

Pro Tip

Keep your oldest credit card active, even if you just use it for a small subscription like Netflix, to maintain your credit history length.

Conclusion

Understanding these myths is the first step to financial freedom. Don't let fear stop you from managing your credit effectively.
